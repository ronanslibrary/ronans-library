# Ronan's Library Substack Email Chain

Use this as a simple welcome sequence for new readers. Substack is strongest when each message is a real post/newsletter, so this chain is written as five publishable emails you can paste into Substack and schedule.

## How To Use This In Substack

1. Create a Substack publication called **Ronan's Library** or **Ronan's Book Club**.
2. Add your Patreon link in the publication profile and in the footer of your posts.
3. Paste each email below as a Substack post.
4. Schedule them a few days apart:
   - Email 1: immediately after launch
   - Email 2: 2 days later
   - Email 3: 5 days later
   - Email 4: 8 days later
   - Email 5: 12 days later
5. Keep the audience set to free subscribers unless you are making a paid-only bonus.

Important: Only add people who clearly opted in to hear from you. Do not upload random contacts.

## Email 2: What This Library Is For

Subject: What Ronan's Library is really about

Hi,

I wanted to tell you a little more about what I am building here.

Ronan's Library is part podcast, part blog, and part book club. The goal is not to make reading feel fancy or intimidating. The goal is to talk about books like they matter in real life.

Some posts will be quick:

- What I am reading this week
- A book I cannot stop thinking about
- A short review
- A quote or idea worth saving

Some will go deeper:

- Why a book works
- Why a book disappointed me
- What makes a character memorable
- How a story changes when you reread it

And when the podcast episodes begin, this newsletter will be where I send the notes, links, and extra thoughts that do not fit into the episode.

Thanks for joining early. It means a lot to have people on the shelf before the whole library is built.

Ronan

## Email 3: The First Reading List

Subject: Five books for the first shelf

Hi,

Every library needs a first shelf.

Here are five types of books I think belong on the Ronan's Library starter shelf:

1. A book that made you love reading
2. A book you did not expect to like
3. A book with a world you wanted to live in
4. A book that changed your opinion about something
5. A book you would recommend to someone who has not read in a while

This week, I am thinking about the books that make people become readers in the first place. Not the books people say they should read, but the ones that actually pull them in.

If you want, reply with one book that belongs on your first shelf. I may use some answers in a future post or episode idea.

Ronan

## Email 4: Podcast Preview

Subject: What the podcast will sound like

Hi,

Here is the shape I am imagining for the Ronan's Library podcast.

Each episode will focus on one clear idea:

- A book review
- A reading theme
- A favorite character
- A genre I am exploring
- A conversation with another reader
- A question like, “What makes a book worth rereading?”

The tone should feel relaxed, thoughtful, and real. Less like a lecture, more like sitting down after finishing a chapter and needing to talk about it.

Possible first episodes:

- The Book That Made Me Want a Library
- How To Choose Your Next Read
- Books Worth Talking About Twice
- Reading Slumps and How To Escape Them
- What Makes a Great First Chapter?

The Patreon will be the place for extra updates, behind-the-scenes notes, polls, and maybe future bonus episodes:

https://www.patreon.com/c/RonansBookClub?vanity=user

More soon.

Ronan
